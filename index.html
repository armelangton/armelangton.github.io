Frequently asked questions

What kinds of business problems do you help solve?

I work on the systems behind day-to-day operations: disconnected workflows, underused CRM platforms, manual handoffs, scattered information, inconsistent sales execution, reporting gaps, and repeatable work that may benefit from automation or AI. The starting point is the business process and the people using it, not a predetermined technology.

Where should we start?

If the problem, priorities, or requirements are still unclear, begin with a focused business systems and workflow assessment. It documents the current process, identifies the highest-value opportunities, surfaces technical or data constraints, and recommends a practical sequence for implementation. A clearly defined project may move directly into scoping.

Are engagements fixed fee?

Yes. A standardized assessment has a published fixed fee. Custom implementations are scoped around the approved users, workflows, systems, integrations, assumptions, deliverables, and acceptance criteria, then quoted as a fixed-fee engagement. You approve the full project investment before implementation begins, with no open-ended hourly billing.

What happens if the scope changes?

Small clarifications that remain within the agreed outcome are handled as part of delivery. A request that changes the approved users, workflow, integration, deliverable, or acceptance criteria is documented before work continues. You can approve it as a fixed-fee addition, place it in a later fixed-fee phase, or leave it out of the project.

What is included in an implementation?

The proposal identifies the exact deliverables for that project. Depending on the work, this may include workflow and requirements documentation, system configuration, application or automation development, integrations, testing, launch preparation, user guidance, training, and a defined post-launch support period. Third-party software and usage charges are shown separately when they apply.

Do you work with the software we already use?

Yes. The best answer may be to improve the tools you already own, connect systems that are not working together, replace one weak part of the process, or add a focused application around an existing platform. Discovery includes reviewing the current stack before recommending new software.

More questions
Does every solution need AI?

No. AI is useful when it can interpret information, support a decision, retrieve knowledge, generate a structured result, or coordinate repeatable work with appropriate review. A conventional workflow, CRM improvement, automation, or better interface is often the more reliable answer. I recommend AI only where it improves the business system.

How do you handle testing, accuracy, and human review?

Testing is based on realistic examples and the acceptance criteria agreed for the project. For AI-enabled work, the design defines what the system may do, what information it may use, when a person must review the result, and how exceptions are handled. Higher-risk decisions remain with an authorized person.

Can the solution expand as our needs change?

Yes. The initial version is intentionally scoped around a useful outcome, while the underlying workflows, data structures, integrations, and documentation are organized so future capabilities can build on the same foundation. Expansion is planned as an additional phase rather than quietly added to the original scope.

Who owns the completed work and what costs continue after launch?

You receive the project deliverables, documentation, and training identified in the agreement. Any ownership terms specific to custom code or reusable components are stated before work begins. Hosting, software subscriptions, AI model usage, messaging, data storage, and other third-party services remain subject to their providers' terms and are identified separately from the consulting fee.

Is ongoing support required?

No. Each project includes a defined handoff and support period. If you want continued monitoring, optimization, content updates, user support, or additional improvements, those services can be arranged as a separate project or a clearly defined monthly support plan.
