Process

A clear path from an unclear problem to a useful solution. Each phase produces decisions, components, and documentation that support the next expansion.

  1. DiscoverBegin with conversations to understand the business, the people, the needs, and the problem.
  2. StrategizeAnalyze what was learned, connect the relevant information, and define the right approach.
  3. DesignTranslate the strategy into workflows, messaging, interfaces, content, or solution requirements.
  4. BuildCreate the application, AI solution, CRM experience, automation, workflow, or enablement resource.
  5. DeployTest, prepare for real-world use, launch, gather feedback, and support future iteration when appropriate.

Frequently asked questions

Clear expectations before work begins.

Are all engagements fixed fee?

Yes. The outcome, included work, acceptance standard, schedule, and fee are agreed before the engagement begins. If a new request changes the approved scope, it is treated as a separate fixed-fee expansion—not added as untracked hours.

Where do we start?

Most new relationships begin with the Systems Opportunity Assessment. It creates the requirements, priorities, solution direction, and fixed implementation scope needed to make a responsible build decision.

Do you replace our existing software?

Usually not. The best solution often improves how existing systems work together. A new application or agent may sit alongside a CRM, accounting platform, knowledge source, or operational tool and connect the work between them.

What happens when we need more later?

The original system is designed as a reusable foundation. Additional workflows, integrations, roles, reporting, knowledge, or agent capabilities can be added through separately scoped fixed-fee expansions.

Who owns the finished work?

The agreed deliverables are handed over with the documentation and training included in the scope. Any third-party platforms, model usage, licenses, or hosting remain subject to their providers' terms and charges.

Is ongoing support required?

No. A defined handoff is part of the build. Optional System Care is available when monitoring, maintenance, updates, or a regular improvement cadence would be valuable.

Do you only work on revenue operations?

No. Revenue operations and CRM are deep areas of experience, but Melangton Labs works on operational problems across a business when the work would benefit from a clearer workflow, connected information, a practical application, or an appropriately controlled AI agent.
