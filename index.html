How I Think

I design structure where work gets messy.

When information is scattered, processes are inconsistent, or systems take too many clicks to use, I look for the structure underneath the problem. Whether I'm working in a CRM, building onboarding resources, designing an AI support tool, or mapping a new workflow, my goal is the same: make the work easier to understand, easier to use, and easier to improve.

01

Find the friction

Look for the places where people are guessing, repeating work, missing information, or fighting the process.

02

Map the system

Understand the people, tools, handoffs, data, workflows, and decisions that shape the experience.

03

Build the structure

Create the CRM logic, resources, workflows, documentation, and support systems that make the path clearer.

04

Improve adoption

Refine the system so people can understand it, use it, trust it, and keep improving it over time.

Principles

Good systems are only useful when people can use them.

Clarity beats complexity.

If people cannot understand the process, they will create their own version of it.

Adoption matters.

The best workflow is not the most impressive one. It is the one people can follow consistently.

Visibility changes behavior.

When the right information is easy to find, teams make better decisions with less guesswork.

Fewer clicks matter.

Every unnecessary step creates drag. Better systems reduce the friction people feel every day.

Structure should support people.

Processes should make work easier, not create more work for the people using them.

Build, test, improve.

I like turning rough ideas into usable systems, then refining them as real people start using them.
