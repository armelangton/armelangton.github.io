Case Study

Designing a Scalable Account Prioritization & Enablement Framework

Role: Lead contributor  |  Stack: HubSpot CRM · ZoomInfo · LinkedIn Sales Navigator

Built a scalable sales enablement and CRM prioritization framework that connected segmentation logic, compliance tagging, reusable messaging systems, onboarding resources, and outreach workflows across a 25,000+ contact database.

The Problem

As the sales team expanded, the CRM had grown to more than 25,000 contacts across multiple industries, compliance categories, and operational priorities. Reps needed a clearer way to understand account fit, compliance drivers, personalization depth, and outreach strategy without relying on disconnected workflows or tribal knowledge.

The larger challenge was not just lead volume. It was creating a scalable operational system that connected prioritization, messaging, enablement, and execution in a way that reps could realistically use day-to-day.

The System

I developed an evolving enablement framework that connected account tiering, compliance and vertical tagging, prospecting workflows, messaging paths, scripts, snippets, LinkedIn personalization, onboarding resources, and outreach execution into a more unified operational system.

Using HubSpot, ZoomInfo, and LinkedIn Sales Navigator together, the workflow was structured so enablement resources appeared directly inside rep execution instead of existing as disconnected documentation. Scripts surfaced within tasks, approved messaging could be quickly inserted using snippets, LinkedIn personalization aligned to account context, and leadership maintained visibility into outreach activity, workflow consistency, and operational execution across the team.

As the team scaled and became increasingly remote, repeated live training, technical demos, and one-off coaching sessions became difficult to sustain consistently. Many of the systems I built, including onboarding resources, messaging frameworks, objection handling support, CRM workflows, and centralized training materials, were designed to reduce repetitive manual support while giving reps easier access to structured knowledge and enablement resources.
